WECWOLVES

Mission

Women Empowerment Club is a student run organization at MDC Wolfson Campus, focusing on advocating for gender requality. Our aim is to educate students on issues regarding women all over the world, encourage and facilitate civic engagement and political activism, as well as to recognize and celebrate female leaders in the community. We welcome students of all genders, backgrounds and opinions, as such diversity is essential to the richness of our conversations and activities.

Guoda Terleckaite
President
President

Guoda Terleckaite

Guoda Terleckaite is an undergraduate student at The Honors College at MDC Wolfson Campus, seeking a degree in International Relations. She joined Women Empowerment Club as a Historian in 2016 and is serving as a President since Fall 2017. Being raised in Lithuania, in a family with strong and driven women, she was not familiar with gender inequality, nor saw the need for feminism in the 21st century. However, since the beginning of her college career, Guoda has performed extensive research on gender inequality and worked to educate other students on feminism and its necessity. She initiated the creation of WEC’s first ever website, revitalized WEC’s online presence on Instagram and Facebook and organized the 2nd “Meet the Pros” panel, featuring distinguished women from the community. Guoda is passionate about gender leadership gap and its effects on the wage gap, the relationship between femininity and feminism, as well as the portrayal of women in TV and social media.

Gabrielle Montes de Oca

Vice President

Gabrielle Montes de Oca

Gabrielle Montes de Oca is a second year sociology major enrolled in The Miami Dade Honors College. Over the summer, she served as a teaching fellow at Breakthrough Miami, an educational program for low-income, minority children. She worked at the Carrollton location, the only all girls site in the nation. Here she not only taught social studies and yoga, but strived to empower these young girls by creating a safe space where they felt understood and appreciated. Gabrielle hopes to continue her change-making efforts as the Vice President of Women Empowerment Club. She is particularly interested in empowering women through education and fighting against the hyper-sexualization of women in the media and society.

Gabriel Cortez

Gabriel Conte Cortez Martins is a student at the Miami Dade Honors College, majoring in Biochemistry. Gabriel serves as a secretary of WEC and is responsible for keeping minutes and recording attendance of every meeting. He first started working with WEC last year and was in charge of the Breast Cancer Awareness project on campus, which united his passion for medicine and altruism. Sol Maniera
Historian
Historian

Sol Maniera

Sol Maniera is a student at the Honors College at MDC, majoring in political science. She is currently the Historian for the Women Empowerment Club, responsible for managing social media accounts and the advertising of the club and its events. Having a passion for women’s rights, Sol founded a Women’s Empowerment Club at Miami Beach Senior High School. As a Fellow of the Democratic National Committee, she advocated for voter registration of women in lower income brackets in her community during the 2016 election. She is a registered Planned Parenthood volunteer and is specifically concerned with women’s sexual and reproductive rights. In the future, Sol hopes to work towards creating environments where girls can grow up safely and be more empowered and confident.
